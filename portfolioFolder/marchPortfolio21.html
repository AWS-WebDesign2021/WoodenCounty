Welcome!

Welcome to the Pacific Northwest; the landscapes, history, culture, food, and wildlife from Alaska to Northern California to east into Idaho! The PNW region is known for her natural beauty, deep history, and appeal to both the rugged outdoor adventurer and the city cat alike.

This page is meant to be a helpful guide to the regions of Washington State and neighboring Soverign Native territories. Featured are current events from sports, arts, to cultere, and more, as well as the history of the select regions within the Pacific Northwest!

Washington is home to mountain ranges, beaches, plains, valleys, waterfalls, rainforests, and even the most modern of metropolitan cities. Whether you enjoy rock climbing, bar crawling, or both we've got you!

Enjoy the Pacific Northwest, friend.

History

What is known today as Washington State has been home to many peoples long before written record. Prior to the early 1800s, the demographics were comprised of native communites. The 1830s brought foreigners from Europe in search of work, aboundance, and opportunity amidst the region's plentiful forests and natural resources.

Places like Tacoma and Aberdeen served as international hubs for the global timber economy, while the entire Pacific coast bursted with ports for seafood and fishing industries worldwide.

Today, is no different; people from all over the world immigrate and visit Washington to experience Her forest and natural wonders and unique environments. While comprised of people from all over the world, Washington is still home to the Duwamish, Quinault, Yakima, Wanapum, Suquamish, Steilacoom, Squaxin, Palouse, and many more Native communites.

Since early colonization, Native-foreigner relations have led to wars, battles, oppressive systems, and exploitative and genocidal governments today. Irregardless, Resistance and freedom movements are alive and well.

Washington State Parks – "The Washington State Parks and Recreation Commission cares for Washington's most treasured lands, waters, and historic places.
State parks connect all Washingtonians to their diverse natural and cultural heritage and provide memorable recreational and educational experiences that enhance their lives."

Washington Tourism Alliance - "Welcome to Washington State ~ We welcome you to the official travel and tourism website for the state of Washington. Use our maps and the official Washington State Visitors’ Guide to plan your next vacation to the Pacific Northwest."

~Super Cool Seattle Center – "More than 30 cultural, educational, sports and entertainment organizations that reside on the grounds, together with a broad range of public and community programs, create thousands of events on the 74-acre campus and attract over 12 million visitors each year. At Seattle Center, part of Uptown Arts & Cultural District, our purpose is to create exceptional events, experiences and environments that delight and inspire the human spirit to build stronger communities."

Museum of Glass – "Located in Tacoma, Washington, Museum of Glass is a premier contemporary art museum dedicated to glass and glassmaking in the West Coast’s largest and most active museum glass studio. Now in its 18th year, MOG has established a reputation for hosting impactful and engaging artist residencies, organizing and exhibiting nationally traveling exhibitions, and creating unique programs for visitors, all while building a growing permanent collection chronicling the development of modern and contemporary glass."

Hilltop Urban Gardens - Tacoma – "Founded in late 2010 by long-time organizer Dean Jackson, Hilltop Urban Gardens is a community-based urban agriculture, justice, and equity organization in Tacoma, WA. HUG partners with the community to grow healthy food, young people and neighbors. We seek to inspire our community to live with abundance while understanding and interrupting the root causes of economic poverty & wealth accumulation and systems of oppression. We believe growing our own food independence is an important strategy in this process."
