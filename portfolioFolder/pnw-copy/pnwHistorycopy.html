History

What is known today as Washington State has been home to many peoples long before written record. Prior to the early 1800s, the demographics were comprised of native communites. The 1830s brought foreigners from Europe in search of work, aboundance, and opportunity amidst the region's plentiful forests and natural resources.

Places like Tacoma and Aberdeen served as international hubs for the global timber economy, while the entire Pacific coast bursted with ports for seafood and fishing industries worldwide.

Today, is no different; people from all over the world immigrate and visit Washington to experience Her forest and natural wonders and unique environments. While comprised of people from all over the world, Washington is still home to the Duwamish, Quinault, Yakima, Wanapum, Suquamish, Steilacoom, Squaxin, Palouse, and many more Native communites.

Since early colonization, Native-foreigner relations have led to wars, battles, oppressive systems, and exploitative and genocidal governments today. Irregardless, Resistance and freedom movements are alive and well.
