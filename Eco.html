1. Why be a Neighborhood Ecologist

Whether you live in one of Seattle's highest skyrises, one of Wenatchee's densest forests, or a cozy neighborhood in between, the state of the natural environment affects you. Living in a world with basic ecological knowledge will benefit you. Unfortunately European colonization did a number in destorying much of the history, evidence, and knowledge about the Natural world and humans' role in it. Nonetheless, Indigenous wisdom and mindsets of reciprocity have proven their resilince. So now, we have the responsibility of honoring that wisdom and paying homage to the People who have fostered trust and relationships with the land we live on and organisms we live with.

2. So Science, So What

From the seemingly common Crow overhead or the types of trees planted in your yard, all of the funky organisms– including the one reading this right now– are working together through the big game of Life through a series of precious Engergy transfers. As you read this, death and birth are happening from the micro level to the macro level. Learning about the small ways in which you impact these Engergy transfers can have a huge affect on their outcomes. Today, many of us humans have been all too comfortable in out negligence to our roles, impacts, and responsibilities to the reciprocal relationships that sustain our Home Planet. This is actively leading to the demise of the human race, and, more unfortunately because of their lack of say in the matter, the extinction of fellow animals.

3. A Better World

Because of the global indoctrination of white supremacy and capitalism, our planet is experiencing irreparable damage for the duration of human existence. Knowlege guiding our actions is the only way to address the otherwise inevitable doom to humans– rich, poor, big, small, funny, not-funny– all of us.
